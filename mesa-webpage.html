MESA: Text-Driven Terrain Generation Using Latent Diffusion and Global Copernicus Data

MESA is a novel generative model based on latent denoising diffusion capable of generating 2.5D representations of terrain based on the text prompt conditioning supplied via natural language. The model produces two co-registered modalities of optical and depth maps.

Abstract

Terrain modeling has traditionally relied on procedural techniques, which often require extensive domain expertise and handcrafted rules. In this paper, we present MESA - a novel data-centric alternative by training a diffusion model on global remote sensing data. This approach leverages large-scale geospatial information to generate high-quality terrain samples from text descriptions, showcasing a flexible and scalable solution for terrain generation. The model’s capabilities are demonstrated through extensive experiments, highlighting its ability to generate realistic and diverse terrain landscapes. The dataset produced to support this work, the Major TOM Core-DEM extension dataset, is released openly as a comprehensive resource for global terrain data. The results suggest that data-driven models, trained on remote sensing data, can provide a powerful tool for realistic terrain modeling and generation.

Extra Information

  • Methodology Highlights: MESA is a generative model trained on Earth observation data, specifically designed for terrain generation. It leverages a global dataset of reflectance, elevation, and text captions, built upon the Major TOM Core dataset, to train a modified Stable Diffusion 2.1 architecture.
  • Dataset: The model is trained on the Major TOM Core-DEM dataset, an extension of the Major TOM dataset, which includes Copernicus Digital Elevation Model (DEM) data at 30-meter resolution. This dataset provides global and dense coverage of Earth’s landmass, encompassing over 1.3 million 10x10km 2.5D terrains.
  • Terrain Generation: MESA generates 2.5D terrains by adapting the Stable Diffusion 2.1 architecture to accommodate both RGB images and DEM data. The model is trained to simultaneously denoise both modalities, ensuring spatial alignment and semantic coherence.
  • Text Conditioning: The model is conditioned on text captions generated from geographical coordinates, including information about countries, biomes, and geological features. This allows for text-driven generation of diverse terrains.
  • Global Coverage: The training dataset covers most of Earth’s land surface, enabling the model to generate a wide variety of terrain types and geographical regions.
  • Qualitative Results: The paper includes qualitative results demonstrating the model’s ability to generate diverse and realistic terrains in response to different text prompts.
  • Potential Applications: MESA has potential applications in creative pipelines for video games and visual effects (VFX), where terrain modeling is a critical component.
